# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

B2B wholesale buyers and apparel/retail brands sourcing bulk knitwear manufacturing (boys', girls', ladies', men's knit garments) from Pakistan. They are evaluating manufacturing partners on trust, capability, and compliance — not browsing as retail consumers. Context: researching on desktop during supplier vetting, often arriving via search or a direct referral, looking to quickly confirm certifications, production scale, and how to get in touch for a quote/inquiry.

## Product Purpose

A modern rebuild of Haram Textile's public website (Faisalabad-based knitwear manufacturer/exporter) plus an admin CMS so staff can edit products, certifications, hero content, and contact submissions without touching code. Success = a B2B buyer trusts the company's scale and credibility within the first screen, can find certifications and product categories within two clicks, and can submit an inquiry confidently. The site must also rank on Google and be citable by AI answer engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, AI Overviews) for B2B apparel-sourcing queries.

## Brand Personality

Trustworthy, Precise, Editorial. The legacy site (haramtextile.com) is corporate-industrial: black/gray/white, carousel hero, stat-box icons, "View More" links — functional but forgettable, indistinguishable from any generic manufacturer site. The rebuild's job is to keep the same operational substance (certifications, capacity, compliance) but present it with editorial-fashion-brand craft (think Loro Piana, Arvind Fabrics) — structured grids, strong typographic hierarchy, full-bleed photography, generous whitespace — so scale and trust read as premium rather than purely transactional.

## Anti-references

Explicitly avoid (from the locked brief): purple/blue gradients, card-in-card nesting, rounded-square icon tiles, floating shadows everywhere, glassmorphism, dashboard-style stat cards, Inter font, generic SaaS layouts, centered-text-plus-two-buttons hero sections, symmetric two-column "About" layouts. Also avoid the legacy site's own corporate-industrial flatness (icon stat-boxes, generic carousel, "View More" link-only product browsing) — the rebuild should read as a step up from that, not a re-skin of it.

## Design Principles

1. **Editorial over corporate** — asymmetric grids, full-bleed imagery, text-over-image with cream overlays; never centered-text-plus-buttons.
2. **One accent, used deliberately** — muted gold (`#B8963E`) is reserved for certifications, MOQ badges, and CTAs; it must not become a decorative tint applied everywhere.
3. **Real photography over stock** — use the company's actual 236 legacy product photos wherever available; only fall back to generic placeholders where no real photo exists (hero/about/client-logo slots).
4. **Trust is stated, not just implied** — every page has at least one direct factual paragraph (who, what, where, credibility) for both human scanners and AI answer-engine extraction.
5. **Scale shown through structure, not stat-box icons** — capacity/certifications/compliance facts get typographic weight and real photography, not rounded icon tiles.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum across all public pages, with particular attention to the brand palette's lower-contrast pairings (green-on-cream, gold-on-green, cream-on-deep-brown). Standard `prefers-reduced-motion` support once motion is added (Phase 4 animation pass); no additional named accessibility user base beyond standard AA practice.
